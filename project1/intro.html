a short reflection

date: oct. 15th, 2025

as my time at berkeley comes to an end, i've had some time to think about how much i've grown in the past three years. in comparison to my pre-college self, i can confidently say that i've changed so much in all directions that i can hardly recognize freshman year self. i've pushed myself harder than i ever thought i could, made friends with people i didn't think i could get along with, and i've come to embrace the uncertainty that my future holds. i think a few things have been constant. i still get anxious at times. i have more or less the same sense of humor. but almost everything else about me then has been shaped by the people around me and the obstacles i've faced on the way.

i think one big thing that i've developed while in college is that desire to constantly grow and improve myself. saying that i'm never satisfied with myself is putting it a little harshly, but i hardly feel like i'm in a spot where i can just relax and be content with where i am. this isn't necessarily a negative thing, because i really do think the ambition i have to remain active at all times has helped me learn so much, gain valuable experience, and make many cool friends. but i have found myself wondering when, if ever, i'll reach a point where i feel like i've mastered something, whether it's in a professional environment or anything lifestyle related. i think part of it is out of my desire to make the most of this precious time in my life where i can make mistakes and explore all sorts of things before locking in on a specific path, so the anxiety of settling on one thing for the rest of my life probably plays a part. but i do hope that i'm stil able to learn new things all the time, even after graduating.

anyways, just a few thoughts as i'm sitting in doe at 1:27 am. thanks for reading!
